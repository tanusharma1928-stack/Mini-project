How ShieldAI processes human language into actionable data.

The Natural Language Processing (NLP) Pipeline

Before the AI can "read," the text must undergo a rigorous cleaning process called Preprocessing.

1. Tokenization & Cleaning

The raw sentence is broken down into individual "tokens" (words). We remove special characters, emojis, and punctuation that don't contribute to the sentiment analysis. Everything is converted to lowercase to ensure "Bad" and "bad" are treated the same.

2. Stopword Removal

Common words like "is", "the", "and", and "a" are removed. These words appear frequently in both safe and bullying messages, so they act as "noise." Removing them allows the model to focus on high-impact keywords.

TF-IDF Vectorization

Machine Learning models cannot understand English letters; they only understand numbers. We use Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency (TF-IDF).

  • Term Frequency: How often a word appears in a specific message.
  • Inverse Document Frequency: How unique a word is across the entire dataset.

This creates a "feature vector" where hurtful words that are specific to bullying are given higher mathematical weight.

Text $\rightarrow$ [0.12, 0.0, 0.85, 0.44]

Mathematical Representation

Support Vector Machine (SVM)

Our engine uses a Linear SVM Classifier. It works by finding the "Hyperplane" that best separates two classes of data: Bullying and Non-Bullying.

High Accuracy

Effective in high-dimensional spaces (like text data).

Real-time Speed

Predictions happen in milliseconds after the user clicks 'Scan'.

Memory Efficient

Uses a subset of training points (support vectors) in the decision function.

Continuous Improvement

Every message analyzed contributes to the system's history. By reviewing the Dashboard metrics, we can identify new trends in digital language and retrain the model to adapt to modern slang and evolving patterns of online harassment.
